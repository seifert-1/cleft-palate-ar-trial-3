Cleft Palate Augmented Reality Model

On an Apple or Android device, tap "View in AR" below to place the model in the room with you. Alternatively, on a desktop web browser, click and drag to rotate in the 3D viewer below.

Mobile devices: press with one finger to rotate the model, pinch with two fingers to zoom and press with two fingers + drag to move the position of the model.

Desktop web browsers: hold the left-click + drag to rotate the model, scroll to zoom and hold the shift button + left-click + drag to move the position of the model.

What you're looking at:

This model shows a complete unilateral left cleft palate on a skull model. The area highlighted in orange demonstrates where the bone did not fully join together before birth, which has resulted in the defect in the left maxilla and left palatine bone.

How to use this:

Drag with one finger to rotate the model. Pinch to zoom. On a phone or tablet, tap "View in AR" to place a life-size version of the model in the room in front of you.

Research project:

This model was created for the research project "The Use of 3D Printed and Augmented Reality Models as Educational Aids in Cleft Care: A Comparative Study" under Adelaide University. The aims of this project are to investigate the preferences of patients and clinicians when offered traditional paper-based pamphlets, augmented reality models and 3D printed models. The 3D model was crafted in the application Blender to render the 3D model of a skull with left unilateral cleft palate depicted above. This model will be 3D printed and used as the 3D printed educational tool shared with clinicians and patients.
